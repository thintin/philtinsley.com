Let’s work out a deal that works for you

Most consultants hand you a price list. I’d rather start with a conversation. Depending on what you need, how your organisation works, and where you are right now, there are several ways we can structure an engagement — and I’m open to creative arrangements that make sense for both sides.

Fixed Fee

A clearly scoped project with a set price and defined deliverables. You know exactly what you’re paying before we start, and I’m accountable to delivering what we agreed. Good for one-off pieces of work with a clear outcome.

Monthly Retainer

Ongoing strategic support on a regular basis — typically one or two days per month. Predictable cost, priority access, and continuity of knowledge built over time. Well suited to organisations going through sustained change who need a consistent, experienced voice in the room.

Deferred Fee

If budget is tight right now but the work needs doing, we can agree to defer part or all of my fee until you’ve had time to see the benefits — or until a funding decision lands. This is how I work with organisations where the timing is right but the cash flow isn’t.

Profit & Gain Share

For the right projects, I’ll tie my fee to measurable outcomes. If the work saves you time, reduces costs, or grows your revenue, we share in that success. This aligns my incentives directly with yours — I only do well when you do.

Milestone-Based

Payments tied to delivery stages rather than time or a lump sum upfront. Keeps both of us accountable to making progress — and means you’re not paying for work that hasn’t happened yet. A good middle ground between fixed fee and retainer.

Day Rate

For shorter engagements, advisory work, or situations where the scope is hard to define upfront. Transparent, flexible, and easy to scale. You use what you need, and there’s no long commitment on either side.

Not sure which model fits? Start a conversation and we’ll figure it out together.

DSPT Standards Met Programme

£1,950 + VAT

The Data Security and Protection Toolkit is an annual NHS self-assessment that every CQC-registered adult social care provider must complete by 30 June. Reaching “Standards Met” — not just “Approaching Standards” — is increasingly required for NHS contracts, scrutinised during CQC inspections, and a condition of many data sharing agreements.

What’s included

  • Gap analysis against all 42 mandatory evidence items (Category 3)
  • Evidence mapping across the 10 National Data Guardian standards
  • Policy and documentation support tailored to your organisation
  • Step-by-step guidance through the DSPT portal
  • Pre-submission review before you publish
  • 30-day post-submission support included as standard

Who it’s for

Adult social care providers — care homes, domiciliary care, and supported living services — who need to achieve or maintain Standards Met. Particularly valuable for providers who have previously sat at Approaching Standards, are new to the DSPT, or have a contract renewal or CQC inspection on the horizon.
